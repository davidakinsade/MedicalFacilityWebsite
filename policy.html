Privacy & Confidentiality

Fionn Medical Centre wants to ensure the highest standard of medical care for our patients. We understand that confidentiality is a fundamental principle of medical ethics and is central to the trust between patients and doctors.

The privacy practices we adopt are in line with:

  • Medical Council Guidelines
  • Data Protection Acts
  • ICGP Guide to Data Protection Legislation

Managing Your Information

  • We collect and keep necessary patient information to provide care.
  • We are committed to retaining your data securely and confidentially.
  • We explain any data collected, and ask you to update us with relevant changes.
  • All staff are bound by confidentiality agreements and professional codes.
  • Patient records are only accessed when necessary to carry out specific duties.

Examples of Staff Accessing Patient Records

  • Printing and preparing repeat prescriptions (approved by GP).
  • Typing referral letters and handling communication with hospitals.
  • Managing results and scanning reports into your record.
  • Checking preventative service needs (e.g., smears, vaccines, check-ups).
  • Handling documents for legal or insurance use under strict procedures.

If you have questions about how your data is handled or want to request access to your health record, please contact our reception team or your GP directly.
